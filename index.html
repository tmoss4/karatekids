Science

Site

Summary

The Map of Science (The Donut of Knowledge)

This video explores what makes a science a science, and how the scientific method is used in different subjects. All of the areas of human endeavor are laid out including how they relate to each other from philosophy, through the sciences, to the social sciences, the arts and back to philosophy. The main theme is that the scientific method is harder and harder to apply to subjects as they deal with more and more complex systems.

The Map of Mathematics

This shows how pure mathematics and applied mathematics relate to each other and all of the sub-topics they are made from.

The Map of Biology

Biology is the subject that studies life in all its forms, from the simple cell through to all the animals that inhabit the planet. In this map the subject of biology and how all of the sub-disciplines are related to each other is explored including: microbiology, genetics, bioengineering, anatomy, physiology, medicine, neuroscience, ecology, and zoology.

The Map of Chemistry

The entire field of chemistry summarised in 12mins from simple atoms to the molecules that keep you alive.

The Map of Physics

Everything we know about physics - and a few things we don't - in a simple map.

The Map of Computer Science

The field of computer science summarised.

How The Internet Works in 5 Minutes

A quick video on the basics of the internet. The internet is not a fuzzy cloud. The internet is a wire, actually buried in the ground. Computers connected directly to the internet are called "Servers," while the computers you and I use are "clients," because they are not connected directly to the internet, but through an Internet Service Provider. Routers shuttle packets of information across the internet, and transmit e-mail, pictures, and web pages.

How Does a Wing Actually Work

A quick video on the basics of flight. Lift is an important concept, not only in flying but also in sailing.

Bite Size Psych

A video on fast and slow thinking.

The 17 Equations That Changed The Course of History

Mathematics is all around us, and it has shaped our understanding of the world in countless ways. Here is a little bit more about 17 wonderful equations that have shaped mathematics and human history.
