Bookmarks

If I have seen further it is by standing on the shoulders of giants. - Issac Newton

This is a collection of some websites I've found over the years that have been uniquely thought-provoking, entertaining, or useful.


Global Knowledge Philosophy Creation Comics Animations Misc

Global

Site

Summary

Ted Talk: Do Schools Kill Creativity?

This is a thought-provoking and hilarious talk by Sir Ken Robinson about why schools around the world teach certain areas as more important than others - along with the consequences of it. 

The Ignorance Project: How Not to be Ignorant About the World

Another entertaining Ted talk this one about what the public doesn't know about basic global realities. Take the test at the beginning of the video and see how much you know. 

The Joy of Stats

If you liked the previous video or are interested in cool applications of math and statistics, check out this video about how Hans Rosling about how it is being used to inform people around the world.

The Good Country Index

The idea of the Good Country Index is to measure what each country on earth contributes to the common good of humanity, and what it takes away, relative to its size. To learn even more, check out this Ted talk about it.

Philosophy

This is Water

If I had to give a commencement speech, this is the one I would give. If you have ever wondered what "teaching you how to think" means then check this one out. People who know how to think are truly educated. 

Desiderata

A poem by Max Ehrmann that I enjoy reading frequently. I often pin it up next to Starry Night in my work area. 

Philosophy Experiments

Collection of online exercises regarding philosophical stances like morality, torture, and the concept of self. 

Talking to God...

Short story about an atheist meeting God on a train. 

Resurrection

Short sci-fi story about life in the future. 

Conceptual Metaphors in Everyday Language

How often do you think you use metaphors in your daily life? If you think it's not often, then I highly suggest taking a look at this paper from The Journal of Philosophy. It turns out our modern language is fundamentally built on the concept of the metaphor and normal conversation would be very difficult without it. 

Creation

W3 Schools

Extensive site about programming in HTML, CSS, Javascript, SQL, PHP, Bootstrap, JQuery, and more.

GitHub

Site for creating and collaborating on code.

Adafruit 

Unique and fun DIY electronics and kits. 

GameSalad 

Drag and drop video game development that allows for some easy projects but has decent power and complexity that will allow for bigger projects. 

ExtraCredits 

Excellent youtube channel by video game designers about designing good video games. I've seen most of their videos. 

Comics

Questionable Content

Ongoing slice of life with a colorful cast of characters, relationship drama, and robots. 

Cucumber Quest

Beautiful from the beginning with a strong stylistic style and upside-down story. 

Alice Grove

From the creator of Questionable Content, this comic is more sci-fi and mystery. 

Sandra and Woo

A webcomic about friendship featuring a young girl Sandra and her pet raccoon Woo. 

XKCD

Stick figure strip featuring humor about technology, science, math, and relationships. 

The Adventures of Business Cat

The only big-business tycoon boasting his own private executive litterbox and luxury mega cat condo. 

ZenPencils

I almost put this one in philosophy because many of them were so good. My favorites are the ones on: Glass advice for beginners, Chris Hadfield, Bill Watterson, Ken Robinson, Amy Poehler, Kevin Smith, Asimov, and Rhodes. 

Animations

RWBY

Have to mention this great webseries. Amazing action sequences and good storytelling. Highly recommended. (The graphics improve immensely). 

Doors

Exceptional collaboration of stick-figure animation by Hyun's dojo. Great animation, great music, and lots of entertainment. Look out for the lightsaber battle<./span>

Ghost - Mystery Skulls

Animated music video that will have you wondering just what's going on. Don't forget to catch the second video in the series, Freaking Out, and another animated video set to a Mystery Skulls song called Magic.  

Animated Short Movies

A collection of short animations with a wide variety of styles. Find lots more on youtube as well. 

Music and Life

Exercpt from Alan Watts about how students are pushed through life and what they are told to believe. 

Misc

StumbleUpon

Input the topics you're interested and jump around the internet to websites related to your interests. This is how I found many of the great sites on this page. I've spent many hours clicking "Stumble" because you never know what you'll find. 

Mathematics (Spoken Word)

This powerful spoken word piece is about the immigration how foreigners to any country affect their surroundings. 

PBS Idea Channel

I picked the video at random but the PBS Idea channel is a great one that discuss a wide variety of topics in pop culture and society. 

Bite Size Psych

A video titled "The simple riddle that 50% of Harvard students get wrong". Click-bait-y title but a cool video from a youtube channel for anyone interested in a look into the field of psychology. 

Cracked - After Hours

Great mini-series of four friends talking about movies. It's really entertaining, well-written, and well-acted. 

DeviantArt

The world's largest online social community for artists and art enthusiasts. There are so many beautiful pieces and lots of fan-art for just about everything. 

The Writer's Almanac

Daily short poems from a variety of poets. This was my homepage for several years.
